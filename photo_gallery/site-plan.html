Overview

Purpose

Photo Gallery is a simple website focused on browsing curated photo collections in a calm, modern layout. The purpose is to help visitors explore images by theme, understand what each collection is about, and view photos comfortably on both mobile and desktop without distractions.

Audience

The primary audience is anyone who enjoys photography and wants an easy way to browse themed collections. Secondary audiences include classmates and instructors evaluating layout, readability, navigation clarity, and overall design quality for a web project.

Content

Home

Welcome and Purpose Photo Gallery is built for visitors who want to enjoy photography without clutter. The home page explains what the site offers, how the collections are organized, and how to move between pages. Instead of long instructions, the site uses simple language and clear navigation so visitors can start browsing immediately.

Why Visitors Come Here People come to this site to explore curated sets rather than endless scrolling. Each collection has a theme such as landscapes, city scenes, textures, or color studies. The goal is to make the experience feel like a small exhibition where every image contributes to a consistent mood, making browsing relaxing and intentional.

What Questions the Site Answers Visitors often want to know what kind of photos they will see, how collections are grouped, and how to view images comfortably on their device. The home page answers these questions by describing the theme based structure, the focus on readable captions, and a viewing flow that supports both quick scanning and deeper viewing.

How to Use the Site The navigation is intentionally short with only three pages. Visitors can start on Home, move to Gallery to browse images, and open the About page to learn more about the theme choices and content approach. This structure keeps the site manageable and avoids complex features that would distract from the images.

Gallery

Collections and Organization The Gallery page is the main browsing space. Images are presented in a grid that adapts to screen size so visitors can scan quickly on small screens and enjoy more columns on larger screens. Each image includes a short caption that gives context without forcing the visitor to read long blocks of text.

Browsing Experience Visitors typically want two different speeds of viewing. Sometimes they want to move fast and compare multiple photos, and other times they want to focus on one image. The Gallery page supports both by keeping the grid consistent and by allowing a larger view when a visitor selects an image, then returning them back to browsing.

What Makes This Gallery Useful Many gallery pages feel busy or confusing because of inconsistent spacing, unclear captions, or too many categories. This site keeps categories limited, uses predictable layout rules, and maintains consistent typography so the visitor spends attention on the photography rather than the interface.

Information Needed to Browse Visitors do not need accounts or external data. The only information needed is the theme of each collection and a caption per image. This keeps the site fast and reliable and supports hosting as a simple static website, which fits the project requirements well.

About

About the Concept The About page explains the intent of the site: a calm place to view themed photography. It clarifies how themes are chosen, why captions are kept short, and why the design uses strong contrast and generous spacing. This page helps visitors understand the meaning and message of the site without turning it into a blog or a personal profile.

Theme Guidelines Collections are grouped around clear visual ideas such as light, color, composition, and atmosphere. A visitor should be able to glance at a collection and understand the overall direction. This guidance keeps the gallery cohesive and prevents the experience from feeling random, which is common when images are not curated.

Design Principles The site prioritizes readability, consistent alignment, and predictable navigation. Images should be optimized for web so pages load quickly. The design avoids unnecessary decoration so the focus stays on photography. These choices support accessibility and make the experience pleasant on different screen sizes.

Contact and Next Steps The site can include a simple contact line such as an email address or a form link later, but it will remain a static site without databases or external data. Future improvements can include more collections, better captions, and refined layouts based on wireframes, while keeping the site simple and maintainable.
