What to Expect

Every trip includes a guide led safety briefing, fitted personal flotation devices, and a pace that matches the chosen river difficulty. If you are new to rafting, start with a scenic float. If you want adrenaline, choose rapids with higher class ratings.

We recommend arriving 20 minutes early so your group can check in, gear up, and ask questions. Our goal is a smooth experience from parking to paddle out.

Safety Standards

  • Certified guides and regular route training
  • Helmet and PFD fitting for every guest
  • Weather and water condition checks before launch

What to Bring

Dress for water, not for air temperature. Quick dry layers and secure footwear make the biggest difference. Leave valuables at home and bring only what you can keep dry.

  • Quick dry clothing and a warm layer
  • Water shoes or sandals with heel straps
  • Sunscreen, sunglasses with a strap, and water
Tip: If you are unsure, choose comfort and warmth. You will enjoy the river more.
